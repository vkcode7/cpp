TOGAF and Zachman are both enterprise architecture (EA) frameworks, but they play very different roles: TOGAF is mainly a **methodology for doing architecture**, while Zachman is mainly a **schema for organizing architecture artifacts**. [knowledgehut](https://www.knowledgehut.com/blog/it-service-management/togaf-vs-zachman)

***

## TOGAF in a nutshell

TOGAF (The Open Group Architecture Framework) is a comprehensive, process‑oriented framework for developing, managing, and governing enterprise architecture. [cioindex](https://cioindex.com/reference/togaf-vs-zachman/)

- **Main purpose**: Guide you step‑by‑step through how to build and evolve an enterprise architecture and align IT with business strategy. [theknowledgeacademy](https://www.theknowledgeacademy.com/blog/togaf-vs-zachman/)
- **Core feature – ADM**: The Architecture Development Method (ADM) is a cycle of phases (from vision to implementation and governance) that tells you *what to do next* and *how*. [conexiam](https://conexiam.com/togaf-vs-zachman/)
- **Scope**: Covers business, application, data, and technology architectures, plus governance, change management, and stakeholder management. [cioindex](https://cioindex.com/reference/togaf-vs-zachman/)
- **Outputs**: Delivers models, standards, roadmaps, and migration plans; very focused on implementation and change over time. [knowledgehut](https://www.knowledgehut.com/blog/it-service-management/togaf-vs-zachman)

A typical use case: a company wants to standardize and modernize its application landscape; TOGAF gives them a process to assess current state, design target state, define gaps, and plan migration projects. [conexiam](https://conexiam.com/togaf-vs-zachman/)

***

## Zachman in a nutshell

The Zachman Framework is a classification schema (ontology) for describing an enterprise from multiple perspectives using a 6×6 matrix. [knowledgehut](https://www.knowledgehut.com/blog/it-service-management/togaf-vs-zachman)

- **Structure**:  
  - Columns = fundamental questions: **What, How, Where, Who, When, Why**. [conexiam](https://conexiam.com/togaf-vs-zachman/)
  - Rows = stakeholder perspectives: planner, owner, designer, builder, subcontractor, and functioning system. [knowledgehut](https://www.knowledgehut.com/blog/it-service-management/togaf-vs-zachman)
  - Each cell is a **type of model or description** (e.g., “What” x “Designer” might be a logical data model). [conexiam](https://conexiam.com/togaf-vs-zachman/)
- **Main purpose**: Ensure you have a complete and consistent set of architecture descriptions across all viewpoints and concerns. [cioindex](https://cioindex.com/reference/togaf-vs-zachman/)
- **Nature**: It does *not* tell you how to develop architecture; it tells you *how to categorize and check coverage* of architecture artifacts. [linkedin](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/togaf-vs-zachman-comprehensive-guide-choosing-right-enterprise-vcrve)

A typical use case: a large enterprise wants to make sure it hasn’t missed any critical view (e.g., business processes, data models, deployment locations) across stakeholders; Zachman helps map and spot gaps. [cioindex](https://cioindex.com/reference/togaf-vs-zachman/)

***

## Key differences

| Aspect | TOGAF | Zachman |
|--------|-------|---------|
| Type | Full EA methodology and framework | Taxonomy / ontology for EA artifacts |
| Focus | Process of developing and governing architecture | Classification of viewpoints and models |
| Main artifact | Architecture Development Method (ADM) lifecycle | 6×6 matrix of perspectives vs. questions |
| Guidance | Detailed “how‑to” (phases, steps, techniques) | Little/no process guidance; mainly structure |
| Scope | Business, data, app, technology, implementation, change | Descriptive coverage of “What/How/Where/Who/When/Why” |
| Typical use | Running EA programs, roadmaps, governance | Ensuring completeness and communication across views |

 [visual-paradigm](https://www.visual-paradigm.com/guide/togaf/togaf-vs-zachman-framework/)

## How they can work together

Many organizations don’t choose *either/or*; they use **TOGAF for the process** and **Zachman for structuring artifacts**. [knowledgehut](https://www.knowledgehut.com/blog/it-service-management/togaf-vs-zachman)

- TOGAF ADM phases drive what you do and when (e.g., “do Business Architecture now”). [conexiam](https://conexiam.com/togaf-vs-zachman/)
- Zachman’s matrix tells you what viewpoints/models you should populate for that phase (e.g., for Business Architecture, fill relevant “What/How/Who/Why” cells for the Owner/Designer rows). [cioindex](https://cioindex.com/reference/togaf-vs-zachman/)

This combination gives both a practical method (TOGAF) and a rigorous coverage/checklist (Zachman) for enterprise architecture.


# Compare TOGAF and Zachman to FEAF

TOGAF, Zachman, and FEAF are all enterprise architecture frameworks, but they emphasize different things: TOGAF = **method**, Zachman = **taxonomy**, FEAF = **federal reference model set**. [cioindex](https://cioindex.com/reference/comparison-of-top-four-enterprise-architecture-frameworks/)

***

## Quick definitions

- **TOGAF (The Open Group Architecture Framework)**  
  A general‑purpose EA framework with a detailed, iterative Architecture Development Method (ADM) used across many industries. [cioindex](https://cioindex.com/reference/togaf-vs-feaf/)

- **Zachman Framework**  
  A 6×6 matrix taxonomy for classifying architecture artifacts by stakeholder perspective (planner → worker) and by question (What/How/Where/Who/When/Why). [cioindex](https://cioindex.com/reference/comparison-of-top-four-enterprise-architecture-frameworks/)

- **FEAF (Federal Enterprise Architecture Framework)**  
  A U.S. federal government EA framework built around standard **reference models** to improve interoperability, shared services, and performance across agencies. [cms](https://www.cms.gov/data-research/cms-information-technology/enterprise-architecture/federal-enterprise-architecture-framework)

***

## Structural focus

| Aspect | TOGAF | Zachman | FEAF |
|-------|-------|---------|------|
| Main shape | ADM lifecycle + content framework | 6×6 matrix (perspectives × questions) | Consolidated Reference Model (CRM) with multiple reference models |
| Core components | ADM, Content Framework, Enterprise Continuum | Cells describing types of models/views | Performance (PRM), Business (BRM), Service (SRM), Technical (TRM), Data/Information (DRM/IRM) etc. [cioindex](https://cioindex.com/reference/togaf-vs-feaf/) |
| Orientation | Process and governance | Classification and completeness | Standardization and interoperability |

- TOGAF tells you *how to develop architecture over phases*. [ardoq](https://www.ardoq.com/knowledge-hub/togaf)
- Zachman tells you *how to categorize and check coverage of views*. [cioindex](https://cioindex.com/reference/comparison-of-top-four-enterprise-architecture-frameworks/)
- FEAF tells U.S. agencies *how to align to common reference models and standards*. [changeis](https://changeis.com/innovategov/basics-of-the-federal-enterprise-architecture-framework/)

***

## Methodology vs reference models

- **TOGAF**  
  - Strong, explicit methodology (ADM) with iterative phases: vision, business, data, application, technology, migration, implementation, governance. [cioindex](https://cioindex.com/reference/togaf-vs-feaf/)
  - Highly flexible and customizable for different organizations and sectors. [cioindex](https://cioindex.com/reference/togaf-vs-feaf/)

- **Zachman**  
  - No development process; it’s a schema/ontology for EA artifacts. [cioindex](https://cioindex.com/reference/comparison-of-top-four-enterprise-architecture-frameworks/)
  - Often combined with another framework (e.g., TOGAF) to give it a process. [entasispartners](https://www.entasispartners.com/blog/understanding-enterprise-architecture-frameworks-a-comparative-guide)

- **FEAF**  
  - No single ADM‑style process; instead, relies on its **reference models** and associated guidance. [changeis](https://changeis.com/innovategov/basics-of-the-federal-enterprise-architecture-framework/)
  - Agencies use the reference models as a common language to build their own architectures and investment decisions. [cms](https://www.cms.gov/data-research/cms-information-technology/enterprise-architecture/federal-enterprise-architecture-framework)

***

## Target domain and usage

| Dimension | TOGAF | Zachman | FEAF |
|----------|-------|---------|------|
| Primary audience | Any industry, global | Any industry, often large/complex orgs | U.S. federal government (and related public-sector orgs) [cioindex](https://cioindex.com/reference/togaf-vs-feaf/) |
| Typical usage | Corporate EA, digital transformation, IT strategy alignment | Documentation, completeness checks, communication; EA “grid” | Cross‑agency standardization, shared services, performance mgmt. [cms](https://www.cms.gov/data-research/cms-information-technology/enterprise-architecture/federal-enterprise-architecture-framework) |
| Flexibility | High; generic and adaptable [cioindex](https://cioindex.com/reference/togaf-vs-feaf/) | High; taxonomy can overlay any method | Lower; prescriptive to federal standards, OMB policy, interoperability needs [cioindex](https://cioindex.com/reference/togaf-vs-feaf/) |

- TOGAF is usually the default choice for commercial organizations wanting a full EA method. [entasispartners](https://www.entasispartners.com/blog/understanding-enterprise-architecture-frameworks-a-comparative-guide)
- Zachman is often used as a *lens* on top of TOGAF/FEAF to structure artifacts. [entasispartners](https://www.entasispartners.com/blog/understanding-enterprise-architecture-frameworks-a-comparative-guide)
- FEAF is chosen when you must comply with U.S. federal architecture and reporting expectations (e.g., agencies, some contractors). [linkedin](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/tahazamzam_while-togaf-is-the-common-used-internationally-activity-7333703287839293441-niYI)

***

## Relationship and how they can combine

- **TOGAF + Zachman**  
  - TOGAF’s ADM drives the lifecycle; Zachman’s matrix is used to ensure that for each phase you populate the right viewpoints (cells). [entasispartners](https://www.entasispartners.com/blog/understanding-enterprise-architecture-frameworks-a-comparative-guide)

- **TOGAF + FEAF**  
  - TOGAF can provide the *process* within an agency, while FEAF’s reference models define the *required standard views and categorizations* for federal reporting and inter‑agency alignment. [goodelearning](https://goodelearning.com/what-is-the-relationship-between-feaf-and-togaf/)

- **Zachman + FEAF**  
  - Zachman can sit underneath FEAF as a more detailed taxonomy for documenting each FEAF domain (business, data, service, etc.), but FEAF remains the compliance and standardization driver. [cms](https://www.cms.gov/data-research/cms-information-technology/enterprise-architecture/federal-enterprise-architecture-framework)

In practice:  
- If you are in private sector → TOGAF (optionally with Zachman for structure).  
- If you are in U.S. federal space → FEAF (often with TOGAF‑style methods internally) and optionally Zachman as a documentation grid. [linkedin](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/tahazamzam_while-togaf-is-the-common-used-internationally-activity-7333703287839293441-niYI)
